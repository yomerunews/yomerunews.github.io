Frequently Asked Questions

What is NHK News Easy?

NHK NEWS WEB EASY (NHKニュースウェブイージー) is a free service by Japan's public broadcaster that rewrites current news stories in simpler Japanese. Articles use basic kanji with furigana readings, shorter sentences, and common vocabulary — making it one of the best resources for Japanese reading practice at the beginner and intermediate level.

How does Yomeru match articles to my level?

Yomeru compares your known vocabulary against the words in each NHK News Easy article. It calculates a comprehension percentage for every article, then ranks them so the easiest ones (where you know the most words) appear first. This way you always have articles you can read with confidence.

Can I use my WaniKani vocabulary?

Yes! Yomeru has built-in WaniKani integration. Click "Import from WaniKani," enter your API token, and Yomeru will automatically pull in all the vocabulary and kanji you've learned. As you level up on WaniKani, just re-import to update your word list.

What JLPT levels does this work for?

NHK News Easy articles are generally aimed at JLPT N4–N3 level, but Yomeru's matching works for any level. If you're N5 it will surface the simplest articles, and if you're N2 or N1 you'll see that most articles are well within your reach. It's a great way to practice reading at any stage.

Is Yomeru free?

Yes, Yomeru is completely free and open source. Your vocabulary data stays in your browser — nothing is sent to any server. You can optionally support the project if you find it useful.
